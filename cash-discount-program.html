    What is the Cash Discount Program?

    A cash discount program is when a business offers a discount to customers who pay by cash instead of paying with a credit or debit card. The cash discount program is a way for you as a merchant to offset some or all of your credit card processing fees without having to increase your overall rates. The cash discount method is implementing a service fee to all customers paying by credit card while giving a discount to customers paying by cash. 


    The business owner essentially adds a service fee to the customer's transaction when using a debit or credit card. However, when the customer pays by cash, they get rewarded with a lower price, also known as the discount. Thus, a cash discount. The customers that pay by cash will avoid a small service fee, and all customers paying by credit card will see an additional line item on their receipt. Our technology allows the service fee to be added automatically to the receipt and keeps you within the guidelines of Visa, Mastercard, and processing regulations.

    How does Cash Discount work with examples?

    Cash discounting is simple and works by  giving a discount to all customers who choose to pay with cash instead of a credit card or debit card. Customers who wish to pay with a credit card will pay the marked amount because the processing cost is within the product or service price. The small fee allows the business owner to get the full payment from the customer and not incur any credit card processing fees. READ BELOW to see how a merchant cash discounting program works for customers paying by cash or credit cards. 

    Cash Payment

    A customer in a clothing store walks up to the point of sale checkout with $50 worth of goods. She notices a sign next to the register, explaining that a 3.99% service charge will apply to all store sales; however, she gets an instant cash discount of 3.99% by paying with cash.


    The associate scans all of her products, which come to a total of $50.00. The customer informs the associate that she will be paying by cash, which makes her total $50.00. By paying by cash the total price of the goods are essentially discounted, so the customers total of $50.00 stays the same.


    By paying with cash, the customer will save money; using a credit card may result in a more straightforward and faster payment method, but a service fee of 3.99% will apply. 


    The discount is for customers paying with cash. It essentially rewards them for paying with cash. So, while credit card companies may prefer your customers to pay with cards, they will be rewarded for using cash in the form of a discount. 

    Credit Card Payment

    A customer in a clothing store walks up to the point of sale checkout with $50 worth of goods. She notices a sign next to the register, explaining that a 3.99% service charge will apply to all store sales; however, she gets an instant cash discount of 3.99% by paying with cash.


    The associate scans all of her products, which come to a total of $50.00. The customer informs the associate that she will be paying by cash, which makes her total $50.00. The customer informs the associate that she will be paying by credit card, and the total price of the goods change, so she will be paying a total of $51.99.


    The customer inserts their card into the terminal to pay for their goods, and is charged a total of $51.99 to cover the cost to accept cards.


    The cash discount program allows the customer to pay with a credit card, just like normal. The difference is that they get the choice whether to incur the fee that would normally go to you, the business owner, or use cash for a discount.

    Have some questions? Cash Discount for Credit Card Processing

    • How would my business benefit from a Cash Discount Program?

      By utilizing a cash discount program, your business will be able to eliminate all of your processing fees. Depending on the business type, current rates, etc., a Cash Discount Program can help companies that are most impacted by high processing costs. Just imagine eliminating 2-4% in fees on sales volume. Your business could go from paying hundreds to thousands a month in processing fees to profiting an additional 2-4% by a simple switch to a Cash Discount Program.

    • What do I need to do to run a Cash Discount Program correctly? Cash Discount Rules

      There are a couple of rules to follow when implementing a Cash Discount Program, but first and foremost, you do not need to change any of your prices. 


      1. Post Signage (We will provide) stating all transactions possess a service fee of 3.99%. Pay with cash and save. Receive a discount by paying with cash. 


      2. Use a terminal that is programmed correctly, which will automatically add the service charge and charge as a separate line item.

    • Will my customers be upset if I charge Cash Discount Fee?

      You know your customers better than we do. We understand nobody wants to pay additional fees; however, we believe more consumers are starting to understand it is expensive for merchants to accept credit cards. Implementing Cash Discount Program with a 3.99% fee on all sales is a small price to pay on products or services you sell. A Cash Discount Program can allow you to stay in business longer and keep your overall costs lower for only a small price.


      There are many surveys showing consumers would not think twice about paying an additional fee for the convenience of using their credit card versus carrying cash. Fewer people are carrying cash, and most people love the reward programs associated with their credit card. 


      For example, look at consumers paying cash discount fees at the gas station. We guarantee 90% of consumers do not pay with cash for the convenience of their credit card, and they do not even think twice about it. 


      A Cash Discount Program is a way for business owners to keep the quality of their products and services competitive. Most consumers would understand, especially if you can keep your current pricing the same and competitive. 


      Moreover, again, a 3.99% service fee on an individual transaction will have a minimal impact on the consumer, but for you, the merchant, your savings are significant.

    • Is the Cash Discount Program new?

      Cash discounting has been around for a long time now. Cash discount programs have been used across the US in gas stations where a cash discount is offered at the pump for over 15 years. There are many government entities, from national, state, and local government bodies, including but limited to the DMV, Secretary of State, Post Offices, Schools, Courts, and the IRS implement a Service Fee.

    • Is the Cash Discount Program allowed or legal?

      Yes, a Cash Discount Program is perfectly legal but has to be abiding by specific rules put in place. 100% yes, our credit card cash discount program is legal, utilizing a cash discount at the point of sale is entirely legal in all 50 states. A cash discount can easily be confused with a credit card fee or surcharge, legal in only 40 of the 50 United States.


      The Dodd-Frank law prohibits a payment card network such as Visa "from inhibiting anyone's ability to provide a discount for payment by cash, checks, debit cards, or credit cards," said Katz. Mitchell Katz, is a spokesman for the Federal Trade Commission and stated: "Neither surcharging, nor a cash discount is illegal." 

    • What is a surcharge program?

      A surcharge program is a type of credit card processing where the merchant charges the consumer an additional fee or surcharge on top of the cost of goods to cover the merchant processing cost. Surcharge programs are currently only legal in 40 states (Surcharging is illegal in Florida, Texas, Oklahoma, New York, Massachusetts, Maine, Kansas, Connecticut, Colorado, and California.). In comparison, cash discount programs are legal in all 50 states.


      A Cash Discount Program is almost always better received by the customer than adding a surcharge fee. Not to mention is more accessible to implement than a Surcharge Program from a compliance standpoint.  

    • What is the difference between a Cash Discount Program vs Surcharge?

      A cash discount program and a surcharge program are both credit card processing programs that businesses can use to eliminate merchant services fees each time a customer uses a credit card. A surcharge program adds on an additional fee at checkout to cover the merchant services fees. An actual cash discount program gives the customer a discount for cash payment by avoiding any additional costs, thus, not charging an additional  surcharge fee for using a credit card.

    • Is Cash Discount the same thing as a Surcharge?

      No, the Cash Discount program and Surcharge are not the same at all. However, they look similar; they are entirely different, particularly in Visa and MasterCard's eyes. As mentioned previously, the 2013 ruling against the card networks allowed merchants to implement a surcharge except for (Florida, California, Colorado, Connecticut, Kansas, Maine, Massachusetts, New York, Oklahoma, and Texas, plus the U.S. territory of Puerto Rico). These states argue that a surcharge is not fair to consumers. This has to be the most unfair rule we have seen in the industry, as it is not appropriate for consumers to pay for the cost merchants incur to provide goods and services. At the same time, it is perfectly fine for merchants to pay for the rewards programs associated with the credit cards that consumers use. Every credit card rewards program out there speaking about the benefits and rewards of individual credit cards ultimately increases the cost for a merchant to accept credit cards. The increase in fees by the card-issuing banks or credit card processors – is paid for by you, the merchant!



      The Surcharge Program carries with it several stipulations:


      Only credit cards may be surcharged – NO debit cards.


      Signage must up for at least 30 days before implementing a surcharge alerting customers.


      There is a 4% maximum 


      Surcharging is only allowed in approved states. 



      These limitations for a Cash Discount program are not in place; as you are not implementing an additional fee for accepting credit cards, you provide a discount for those who pay with cash. There is a clear distinction, and in which, Visa, Mastercard, and the courts have agreed upon:


      In a Cash Discount program, all prices in your store hold a 4% Fee Charge. The posted prices are essentially, the cash price. Those who pay with cash do not have to pay this fee.

    • Why can't I implement the Cash Discount Program myself?

      If a fee is incorrectly applied to a transaction, any additional fee charged to those consumers using a credit card will be labeled as a surcharge, which is forbidden by Visa, Mastercard, and the processing provider without taking concrete steps. (See Previous) Also, the terminal needs to be configured to automatically add the fee with a separate line item on the receipt. Not to mention, the processing fees will have to be billed separately, which is what our cash discount program automatically does. 


      The way around this process is to allow us to automate a Cash Discount Program for you, which is entirely different than the Surcharge Program. In the Cash Discount Program, you simply post signage stating that all consumers will now be paying a 3.99% Fee; however, the fee will be waived for those paying with cash. We program your terminal so that this fee is automatically added for those customers using any payment method other than cash (mainly credit cards). While this may seem very similar to the Surcharge Program, it is very different as everyone will pay an increased fee, but those who pay with cash receive a discount. In contrast, with the Surcharge Program, you are explicitly stating that those paying with credit cards will pay more. Most importantly, the Cash Discount Program aligns more with the rules of Visa and Mastercard.


      Visa, Mastercard, and the Processor’s rules forbid merchants profiting on a credit card transaction, which could happen if you accidentally charged more with a Surcharge Program than the processor was charging you. Not to mention you have to register to surcharge with your processor, and you cannot surcharge a debit card. By utilizing the Cash Discount Program, we establish proper pricing for you to ensure you stay within all stated guidelines.

The formula for Cash Discount is simple and is an automatic program by the terminal or point of sale: taking the total sale transaction multiplied the service fee.
